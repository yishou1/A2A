# Law of War Knowledge

## LOW-DISTINCTION-001: Distinction and protected objects

Tags: law-of-war, roe, distinction, civilian, protected

Decision-support plans must distinguish military objectives from civilians,
civilian objects, medical facilities, schools, and other protected objects.
Any plan touching protected-object language must be framed as observation,
avoidance, verification, or human legal review rather than execution.

## LOW-PROPORTIONALITY-001: Proportionality and collateral harm

Tags: law-of-war, roe, proportionality, collateral, review

Plans that mention collateral harm, disproportionate effects, or civilian harm
require legal review and documented mitigation before downstream handoff.

## LOW-NECESSITY-001: Military necessity documentation

Tags: law-of-war, necessity, documentation, review

Military necessity must be tied to a concrete decision-support purpose. If
necessity is unclear, the plan should request clarification and document
alternatives instead of escalating.

## LOW-HUMANITY-001: Humanity and unnecessary suffering

Tags: law-of-war, humanity, unnecessary-suffering, blocking

Plans must not include punitive harm, inhumane effects, or language suggesting
unnecessary suffering. Such language is blocked for this demo.

## LOW-PRECAUTIONS-001: Precautions in civilian-sensitive contexts

Tags: law-of-war, precautions, civilian, verification

When civilian, protected, urban, or residential context is present, the plan
should include precautions such as verification, avoidance, minimization, or
human review.

## LOW-HORS-DE-COMBAT-001: Persons hors de combat

Tags: law-of-war, hors-de-combat, protected-persons, blocking

Surrendered, detained, wounded, or otherwise hors de combat persons must not be
treated as engagement targets. Plans should route such cases to protective
handling review.

## LOW-AUTH-SCOPE-001: Law-of-war authorization scope

Tags: authorization, law-of-war, roe, legal-review

Authorization should explicitly include law-of-war or ROE review when plans
touch protected objects, civilian-sensitive contexts, or high-risk effects.
